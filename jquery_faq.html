National Park FAQ's

1. Who is the director of the National Parks Services?
Charles F Sams III
2. How many areas are in the National Park System?
The system includes 424 areas covering more than 85 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands.
3. What is the largest national park site?
Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK, at 13.2 million acres
4. What is the smallest national park site?
Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial, PA, at 0.02 acres
5. How many people visit the national parks?
Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2021: 297,115,406.
6. How do I make reservations for camping/lodging in national parks?
For campground reservations, visit Recreation.gov.
7. Can I bring my pet to a national park?
Some national parks welcome pets—in developed areas, on many trails and campgrounds, and in some lodging facilities. Learn more about pets in parks.
8. Can I bring my service dog to a national park?
Yes, service dogs are legally permitted anywhere in a park that visitors can go. Learn more about service animals in national parks, including the difference between service animals and emotional support animals.
9. How do I find out about contracting opportunities?
Opportunities to contract with the National Park Service are posted to FedBizOpps.gov, the federal government’s electronic business portal.
10. What is the National Park Service budget?
FY 2017 Enacted: $2.932 billion
Source: NPS.gov

National Parks

Yosemite National Park

  • On October 1, 1890, Yosemite became a national park, and more than 125 years later, it’s still wowing visitors.
  • Yosemite’s granite rock formations glow like fire at sunset.
  • The park’s diverse landscape supports more than 400 species.
  • Yosemite is home to one of the tallest waterfalls in the world.

Yellowstone National Park

  • Half the world’s hydrothermal features are found at Yellowstone.
  • Sprinkled amid the hot springs are the rarest fountains of all —geysers —and Yellowstone has more than anywhere else on earth.
  • Yellowstone is world-renowned for its bison herds.
  • The earliest intact archeological deposits in the park were discovered at a site on the shore of Yellowstone Lake.

Grand Canyon National Park

  • The Grand Canyon is bigger than the state of Rhode Island.
  • The Grand Canyon itself can influence the weather.
  • The Grand Canyon is one of the most visited national parks in the United States.
  • The Grand Canyon was carved over some 6 million years.

Glacier National Park

  • Glacier National Park is a part of the world’s first international peace park.
  • Glacier straddles the Continental Divide, allowing for extreme weather.
  • Evidence of human use in the area dates back over 12,000 years.
  • Glacier has welcomed over 100 million visitors to the park.
